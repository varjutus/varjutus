1500s-1900s

Ottoman occupation of the Balkan Peninsula, persecution of certain groups

1941-1945

Independent State of Croatia (Nazi Puppet State) persecutes Serbs and other minorities

1945

Yugoslavia is proclaimed as a communist federal state

1963

Federal People's Repuiblic of Yugoslavia is renamed the Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia

1974

New constitutions adopted for Yugoslav federal government and each of its constitutent republics and autonomous regions, granting greater power at the expense of federal powers

1980

Yugoslav President Josip Broz Tito dies

1990

Slovenia and Croatia vote for greater autonomy or independence from Yugoslavia

1990

Ban on ethnic parties is lifted: Serbian Democratic Party, Croatian Democratic Union, and Party of Democratic Action of Bosnia and Herzegovina established; they succeed in elections

1991

Serb nationalists establish Assembly of the Serbian People of Bosnia and Herzegovina; independence referendum held, majority vote to remain within Yugoslavia

1992

Republika Srpska established

1992

Bosnia holds independence referendum; most Croats and Bosniaks vote for independence, Serbs boycott; when results are announced, Serbs react violently, starting the Bosnian War

1992

Lisbon Agreement proposed; inital acceptance but later rejected by Bosniak leaders

1993

Vance-Owen Peace Plam proposed; partial acceptance by all but later rejected (overwhelmingly) by Bosnian Serb Assembly

1993

UN Security Council Resolution 824 establishes five Bosniak enclaves – including Srebrenica – as safe areas under protection

1993

UN Security Council Resolution 824 establishes the International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY)

1993

Owen-Stoltenberg Peace Plan proposed and rejected

1994

NATO begins air strikes against Serb positions

1995

Radovan Karadžić signs Directive 7, instructing the Army of Republika Srpska to "create an unbearable situation with no hope of further survival or life for the inhabitants of Srebrenica and Žepa"

1995

Srebrenica Massacre – 8000 Bosniak men and boys killed, 25000-30000 more displaced

1995

Dayton Agreement negotiations begin

1995

Dayton Agreement signed, violence ends

1995

ICTY indicts Radovan Karadžić and Ratko Mladić and issues international arrest warrants
